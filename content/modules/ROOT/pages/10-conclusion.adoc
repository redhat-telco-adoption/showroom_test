== The master diagnostic workflow

Every failure in this workshop was resolved using a variation of the same four-step approach:

. *Observe*: `oc get pods` to identify which pods are not `Running` and check restart counts.

. *Investigate the pod*: `oc describe pod <name>` to read the Events section, container state, and conditions. This single command resolves the majority of failures.

. *Read the logs*: `oc logs <name>` (and `oc logs <name> --previous` for crashed containers) to see what the application reported before it failed.

. *Check the namespace timeline*: `oc get events --sort-by='.lastTimestamp'` to see the sequence of events across all resources in the namespace.

When no pods exist for a deployment, substitute step 2 with `oc describe replicaset -l app=<name>` to find admission failures.

== Key takeaways

The most important patterns to carry forward:

. *Pod state is a category, not a cause*: `Pending` narrows the problem to scheduling or missing resources. `CrashLoopBackOff` means the container starts but the application exits. `ImagePullBackOff` means the image cannot be pulled. The Events section reveals the actual cause.

. *The Events section is almost always the fastest path*: Every module in this workshop could be diagnosed by reading the Events section of `oc describe pod` or `oc describe replicaset`. Scroll to the bottom of describe output first.

. *Timeout versus refused*: A connection timeout to a ClusterIP service means a NetworkPolicy is dropping the packet. A connection refused means the packet arrived but was rejected. These require different fixes.

. *OOMKilled exit code 137, ImagePullBackOff 401 vs 404*: Specific exit codes and HTTP status codes in error messages tell you exactly which fix to apply without guessing.

. *Right-size requests, not limits*: Resource requests determine scheduling. Limits determine kill thresholds. Setting requests too high starves the scheduler. Setting limits too low causes OOMKilled loops.

. *Non-root by default*: OpenShift's `restricted` SCC enforces non-root execution. Remove `runAsUser: 0` from any deployment copied from a Docker or VM environment. For volume permissions, use `fsGroup` instead of running as root.
